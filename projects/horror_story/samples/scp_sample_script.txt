While most children look to the skies for a sleigh and reindeer, families in the cold, rural reaches of the northern hemisphere should be watching the treeline for a naked, emaciated old man carrying a sack made of human skin.

Item number: SCP-4666. Object Class: Keter.

Special Containment Procedures. SCP-4666 is currently uncontained. Foundation assets are to monitor satellite imagery and local law enforcement reports for signs of Weissnacht Events across the northern hemisphere. When a Weissnacht Event is confirmed, Task Force Rho-29, nicknamed "The Carolers," is to be deployed to the location to administer amnestics, suppress media coverage, and recover any instances of SCP-4666-A.

Containment efforts are primarily focused on the suppression of information regarding SCP-4666. Due to the entity's ability to manifest anywhere north of the 40th parallel north, physical containment has proven impossible with current technology.

Description. SCP-4666 is a humanoid entity of unknown origin, appearing as an elderly male of European descent. It stands over two meters tall and is exceptionally emaciated. The entity is perpetually naked, even when active in environments where temperatures drop well below freezing. Despite its frail appearance, it is capable of extreme physical exertion and possesses an anomalous ability to move undetected through heavily wooded or snow-covered terrain.

SCP-4666 is active only during a twelve-night period every year, from the night of December 21st to the night of January 1st. This period is designated as a Weissnacht Event. During these events, the entity targets isolated, rural homes inhabited by families with at least one child under the age of eight.

A Weissnacht Event typically follows a set progression over the course of twelve nights. For the first eleven nights, the targeted family will experience minor anomalous phenomena. These include the sound of movement on the roof or in the attic, footprints in the snow that lead to the house but never away, a distinct odor of rotting meat or wet fur, and sightings of a tall, thin figure watching the house from the treeline.

On the twelfth night, one of two scenarios occurs.

In the first scenario, which occurs in approximately eighty-five percent of cases, SCP-4666 enters the home and murders all occupants. The methods used are brutal and primitive, involving blunt force trauma and dismemberment while the victims are often still conscious. Following the massacre, SCP-4666 will consume portions of the victims and then disappear.

In the second scenario, occurring in the remaining fifteen percent of cases, the entity murders all family members except for one child under the age of eight. This child is then abducted. Following these abductions, a small, crudely wrapped package is often left behind at the scene or at a nearby residence. These packages contain instances of SCP-4666-A.

SCP-4666-A refers to a series of handcrafted toys or household items. These objects are constructed entirely from the biological remains of human children. Forensic analysis of these items consistently identifies the DNA of children who were abducted during previous Weissnacht Events.

To date, the Foundation has documented over five hundred Weissnacht Events dating back to the late eighteenth century, though historical records suggest the entity has been active for much longer.

The following is an excerpt from Incident Report 2018-05, detailing a Weissnacht Event in rural Minnesota.

The Miller family, consisting of two parents and three children, occupied a farmhouse approximately twenty miles from the nearest town. On December 22nd, the father reported to local police that he had seen a "tall, naked man" standing in the field behind their barn. No action was taken by local authorities. Over the next week, the family reported scratching sounds coming from the walls and the discovery of small, barefoot prints in the frost on the outside of their second-story windows.

On the morning of January 2nd, a neighbor discovered the front door of the Miller farmhouse standing open. Foundation investigators arrived shortly after local police. The interior of the house was covered in significant quantities of blood. The parents and the two eldest children were found in the kitchen. They had been bound with rope made of braided human hair and subjected to massive trauma before being partially consumed.

The youngest child, a six-year-old boy named Toby, was missing.

On the porch of the neighboring farmhouse, three miles away, investigators found a small box wrapped in dried skin. Inside was a flute. Testing revealed the flute had been carved from a human femur, and the finger holes had been bored through with a crude tool. Genetic testing confirmed the bone belonged to a girl abducted from a farm in Norway three years prior. The "reeds" inside the flute were made from strips of human tongue. When played, the instrument produces a sound described by investigators as a high-pitched, distorted scream.

The horror of SCP-4666 lies not just in its brutality, but in its craftsmanship. In 1996, an instance of SCP-4666-A was recovered in rural Russia. It was a drum. The frame was made from the ribcage of a young boy, and the drumhead was a single piece of human skin, stretched and cured. The drumsticks were humerus bones tipped with mummified human ears.

Most disturbing is the age of the materials. Some instances of SCP-4666-A use components from children abducted decades apart. This suggests that the abducted children are not immediately killed. Evidence indicates they are kept alive in an unknown location, possibly for years, and used as labor or as raw materials for the entity’s "gifts."

In 2010, the Foundation recovered a "toy" in Alaska that consisted of a small wooden wagon. The wheels of the wagon were not wood, however. They were circular sections of bone, and the "axles" were held in place by dried tendons. The wagon was being pulled by the mummified remains of a small hand. Analysis showed the hand had been severed while the child was still alive, as evidenced by the inflammatory response in the surrounding tissue.

The "Yule Father" does not appear to follow any religious or cultural motivation. It does not target specific ethnicities or belief systems. It simply seeks out isolation and the presence of children. The coldness of the environment seems to be a prerequisite, as no events have ever been recorded south of the 40th parallel.

Foundation efforts to track the entity via GPS during an abduction have failed. In 2015, a child in a high-risk area was outfitted with a subcutaneous tracking chip. On the twelfth night of the Weissnacht Event, the family was killed, and the child was taken. The tracking signal remained active for approximately three minutes, moving at a speed exceeding two hundred kilometers per hour through dense forest, before the signal abruptly vanished. No exit point or spatial distortion was detected.

The psychological impact on survivors is absolute. In the rare cases where a family member survives a 4666 attack, usually by being away from the home during the final night, they report a sense of being watched for weeks leading up to the event. They describe a feeling of "profound, ancient malice."

One survivor, who was hidden in a crawlspace during the massacre of his family, provided a brief description of the entity’s behavior. He stated that the "Yule Father" did not move like a human. It moved with "jerky, spider-like" precision. He claimed the entity spent hours "cleaning" the kitchen after the murders, meticulously arranging the remains of his siblings into patterns on the floor before selecting which parts to eat and which to bag.

The bag itself is a point of significant interest. Witnesses and forensic evidence suggest the sack SCP-4666 carries is much larger on the inside than it appears. It is believed to be the primary method of transport for abducted children.

There is no known way to deter SCP-4666 once a house has been selected. Increased security, lights, and even armed guards have proven ineffective. In one instance, a private security team was hired to protect a wealthy family in a rural estate. On the twelfth night, all four security guards were found decapitated and hung from the trees surrounding the property. The family was processed in the usual manner.

The entity’s name, the Yule Father, is a translation of various regional names given to it by rural communities who have lived in fear of the twelve nights for centuries. In some parts of the Alps, it is referred to as "The Bone Carver." In parts of Siberia, it is "The One Who Brings the Red Snow."

The Foundation continues to investigate the possibility that SCP-4666 is part of a larger ecological or extra-dimensional phenomenon. The craftsmanship of SCP-4666-A shows a level of intelligence and intent that goes beyond simple predatory behavior. It is making something. It is participating in a ritual that has remained unchanged for millennia.

As the winter solstice approaches, Foundation personnel are placed on high alert. We monitor the snow. We watch the treelines. We listen for the scratching on the glass. But for those living in the vast, empty spaces of the north, there is little that can be done. If you hear footsteps on your roof this December, or if you find a small, barefoot print in the snow outside your child’s window, do not look for a man in a red suit.

It is already too late.
